# festack profile

Written to `$FESTACK_HOME/profile.md`. Read on demand by festack skills. This is the single place company, stack, and personal specifics live, so the skills themselves stay vendor-neutral. Fill what you know, leave the rest blank. `/learn` deepens it over time from real engagements.

## Identity

- **Name.**
- **Role.** (Solution Architect, Field Engineer, Sales Engineer, etc.)
- **Company / vendor.** The product or platform you represent. This is the thing that is hardcoded nowhere else.

## What you sell or support

- **Platform / stack.** The technologies you demo, position, and support.
- **Core capabilities.** The handful of things you most often show or defend.
- **Differentiators.** What you win on against the usual alternatives. (Used by `/compete`.)
- **Named competitors / alternatives.** The companies, products, and incumbent patterns you most often compare against. (Used by `/compete`.)
- **Proof points.** Case studies, benchmarks, reference architectures, product docs, or recurring evidence categories that support the differentiators.

## Research resources

Where the skills should look first when researching a client or product question. Keep these as named sources, the skills supply the how.

- **Public docs.** URLs or doc sites.
- **Internal knowledge base.** What it is and how to reach it.
- **Chat / community.** Channels or spaces worth searching.
- **Other.** CRM, ticketing, wikis.

## Audience defaults

- **Typical client audience.** Who is usually in the room, business vs technical.
- **What they care about.** Recurring priorities and objections.

## Delivery defaults

- **Demo platform.** Default asset type and environment (notebook, app, sandbox, slides).
- **Output format.** Your document target for written deliverables. Use local markdown only if you explicitly want local files as the default.
- **Canvas.** On by default for understanding and deliverables.
- **Document writer adapters.** Skills or tools that can create, edit, share, and format written deliverables.
- **Diagram adapters.** Skills or tools for diagrams, icon packs, editable handoff, screenshots, and export formats.
- **Build adapters.** Skills or tools that can build approved demo or POC assets, validate setup, run checks, and export or deploy.
- **Verification defaults.** The checks you expect before something is called done: tests, preview, screenshot, export, deployment status, or stakeholder review.

## Capability setup hints

Named sources and adapter families that `/setup-capabilities` can turn into provider mappings. Keep these as hints, not hard workflow rules.

- **Account research providers.** CRM, internal search, account notes, meeting transcripts, or account planning systems.
- **Product-answer providers.** Public docs, internal docs, support knowledge, product channels, or expert lookup.
- **Usage and risk providers.** Consumption metrics, telemetry, ticketing, blockers, support escalations, or incident sources.
- **Document and diagram providers.** Google Docs, docs-canvas, Figma, FigJam, tldraw, Mermaid, Lucid, Draw.io, or other artifact tools.
- **Build providers.** Apps, notebooks, bundles, deployment systems, sandboxes, or local dev loops.
- **Capability setup status.** Blank until `/setup-festack` or `/setup-capabilities` writes the provider registry.

## Voice profile

Read by the `fe-deslop` skill. Overrides its defaults.

- **Tone.** (casual-but-sharp, warm-professional, dry-technical, etc.)
- **Sentence style.** short | mixed | long
- **Contractions.** always | sometimes | never
- **Hedging.** Do you hedge when unsure, or state directly?
- **Humor.** none | dry | overt
- **Formality.** casual | professional | formal
- **Em-dashes.** allow | ban (default ban)
- **Custom banned phrases.** Words you never want to see, on top of the fe-deslop list.
- **Writing samples.** 2 to 3 paragraphs of your own writing. The strongest input for voice matching.
