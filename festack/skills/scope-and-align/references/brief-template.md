# engagement brief template

The written brief. Client-credible, not internal notes. Lead with the recommendation. Keep decided items separate from open ones. Short declarative sentences. No em-dashes. No mid-sentence colon connectors.

---

# [Engagement name]: engagement brief

**Date.** [date]
**Prepared by.** [SA name]
**Status.** Aligned / Pending decisions

## Recommendation
The winning deliverable and the one-line reason it wins. State this first.

## Vision
The end state the client wants. Two sentences.

## Goal
The specific objective of this deliverable. What changes and for whom.

## Audience to win
The roles that must be convinced, business and technical. What each needs to see.

## Requirements
- Functional: what the solution must do.
- Constraints: platform, data, regulatory, time, budget. Explicit, not assumed.

## Success criteria
The measurable or observable checks both sides agree on. Each with a baseline or target.

## Handoff fields
Use these fields verbatim when passing the brief into `/demo`, `/poc`, `/solution-critic`, `/diagram`, or the delivery agent. Downstream skills inherit them and do not re-ask unless the user contradicts the brief.

- Goal: [settled objective]
- Audience: [roles and what each needs]
- Success criteria: [measurable or observable checks]
- Belief to change: [current doubt the work must resolve]
- Judges: [who decides whether it worked]
- Decision unblocked: [purchase, expansion, migration, approval, or other decision]
- Hard constraints: [platform, data, regulatory, time, budget]

## Context
Current state, incumbents, prior attempts, what they compare us to.

## Risks and mitigations
Each risk with its early signal and its mitigation. Named, not generic.

## Winning deliverable
The chosen artifact and the path it opens. If a sequence, the order and why.

## Decisions made
The forks already resolved, each with the choice and a one-line reason. This stops the team relitigating settled points.

## Open decisions
The genuine decisions still open. Each with the options, the trade-off, the owner, and the date needed.

## Next step
The concrete next action and who does it.
