# compete structure

The structure of a competitive analysis, and the rules that keep it credible. All competitor and differentiator content comes from the profile, never from this skill. festack ships no opinions about who wins.

## Two modes

- **Fast.** A quick chat answer from the profile's differentiator notes. 2 to 3 differentiators, the use-case fit, done. Use for a quick "why us over X" in conversation.
- **Comprehensive.** A researched analysis for shareable competitive claims. If the ask is to create a packaged battlecard, leave-behind, client doc, or proof demo, use `festack-delivery-agent`; `/compete` supplies the competitive research and claim discipline.

Pick fast unless the ask signals comprehensive.

## Comprehensive document structure

1. **Quick answer.** The 30-second version. If they read one paragraph, this is it.
2. **Comparison.** Feature or capability comparison, scaled to what matters for this client. A fair table, not a hit piece.
3. **Where we win.** 3 to 5 differentiators, each with a specific reason and proof, not a slogan.
4. **Where the competitor is strong.** Stated honestly. Understating their strengths destroys your credibility the moment the client knows better.
5. **Use-case fit.** When each option is the right call. Naming where the competitor fits builds trust.
6. **Objection handling.** The questions the client will raise, with straight answers.
7. **Proof points.** Customer examples, benchmarks, references. Profile-supplied.
8. **Next steps.** The demo, POC, or trial that would settle it.
9. **Sources.** Every claim cited.

## Credibility rules

- **Be fair.** State the competitor's real strengths. A one-sided analysis reads as marketing and loses the technical buyer.
- **Be specific.** Differentiators are concrete capabilities with proof, not adjectives. Strip the superlatives on the `fe-deslop` banned list; a capability with evidence beats any adjective.
- **Cite everything.** Every comparison claim links to a source. Weight by authority and recency.
- **Stay current.** Competitive facts go stale fast. Flag anything you could not freshly confirm.
- **No invented gaps.** If you cannot prove a competitor limitation, do not assert it.
